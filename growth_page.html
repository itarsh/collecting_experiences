For the woman looking for growth, creativity and new experiences.

London is teaming with experiences for you to get involved in, however we all live fast-paced lives and sometimes it's difficult to know where to look. That's why we've done all the hard work for you and collected the top growth experiences all in one convenient place, so you can get on with becoming the best version of yourself.

Use your free time to meet likeminded people, expand your skillset, find a hobby and even improve your career prospects! Independent women, it's time to invest in yourself this 2020.

#GIVEBACK

Childline, Volunteering Roles

Childine are a brilliant charity that have been running since 1986, offering a free and confidential 24 hour helpline for young people. What ever your time commitment, Childline has a role for you, offering free training for dedicated volunteers. Studies have shown that volunteering has a positive impact on your well-being, so why not start volunteering today to learn a new skill and change a young person's life.

Volunteer roles include: Switchboarders, Administrators and Online and Telephone Counsellors.

CodeFirst Girls, Coding Course

Women currently only represent 17% of the technology workforce. Tech shouldn’t just be a boys club, so why not try one of CodeFirst Girls free courses and see what tech has to offer you.

Coding can be fun, creative and empowering. Get ready to be surprised at your own ability to understand the mysteries of HTML, CSS, Javascript and Python. The helpful mentors and friendly like minded girls that come to this course will turn a coding class into an enjoyable evening you look forward to each week!

Course competitions, free food, great company, and a variety of dates and locations to choose from; this opportunity is not to be missed!

London School of Economics, Free Educational Talks

Looking for inspiration or to spend an evening broadening your mind? LSE offers free educational talks every night of the week in Central London.

From theories of feminism to the philosophy of music; LSE has a thought-provoking talk to provide new perspectives and caters to all intellectual tastes. Listen and absorb the work of inspirational women from around the world and see ‘What’s On’ this week!

Looking for empowering stories with a female voice? Don't miss LSE’s 'Sisters Doing it For Themselves' talk on 18th March, exploring the oral histories of key female figures that shaped the voluntary and community sector.

Toastmaster, Public Speaking Masterclasses

For centuries women have been told to be ‘seen and not heard’. Don’t be too loud. Don’t take up too much space. Toastmasters is an international society dedicated to empowering women and improving overall confidence. Improve your public speaking skills, build leadership skills and maximise your potential in a small and supportive environment.

Toastmasters have clubs all over London, so have a look around and find the perfect club for you!
